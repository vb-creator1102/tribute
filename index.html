Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

1875-1950

Iron man of India

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was born on 31st October 1875 in Gujarat, India. He was a key leader in India's struggle for independence. Known as the "Iron Man of India," he united 562 princely states. Patel played a crucial role in the integration of India post-independence. He was the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of India. His leadership in the Bardoli Satyagraha earned him the title "Sardar." The Statue of Unity, the world's tallest statue, honors his legacy. Sardar Patel passed away on 15th December 1950, leaving a lasting impact. Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was a prominent leader in the Indian freedom movement. He was born in Nadiad, Gujarat, and studied law in England. Patel successfully led movements like Kheda and Bardoli Satyagraha. He was called the "Bismarck of India" for his efforts in uniting the nation. He worked closely with Mahatma Gandhi during the independence struggle. Patel firmly believed in national integration and strong governance. He played a major role in framing the administrative foundation of India. Today, National Unity Day (October 31) is celebrated in his honor.
